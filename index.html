Richard Feynman

Physicist, Nobel Prize Winner, Lover of Life

Professor Feynman showing off his skill with the bongos

Richard Phillips Feynman was an American theoretical physicist, known for his work in quantum mechanics, the theory of quantum electrodynamics, and for introducing the concept of nanotechnology.

He was a keen popularizer of physics through both lectures and books, including his semi-autobiography Surely you're joking, Mr. Feynman!.

  • 1918 - Born in Queens, New York City
  • 1939 - Receives bachelor degree, enters Princeton University with perfect scores in physics
  • 1941 - Joins the Manhattan Project after the Pearl Harbor attack
  • 1945 - Becomes Professor of Theoretical Physics at Cornell University
  • 1948 - Publishes 'Feynman Diagrams', a representation of mathematical sequences of subatomic particles, contributing to the understanding of quantum field and solid-state theories
  • 1950 - Appointed Professor of Theoretical Physics at the California Institute of Technology
  • 1964 - The Feynman Lectures of Physics are published, one of the most popular physics textbooks
  • 1965 - Is lauded with the Nobel Prize in Physics for work on quantum electrodynamics
  • 1972 - Wins the ‘Oersted Medal’ for being an outstanding teacher of physics
  • 1979 - Wins the ‘National Medal of Science’ for contributions to physics
  • 1986 - Investigates the explosion of NASA space shuttle Challenger, and ascertaines that the fatal explosion was caused due to low launch day temperatures
  • 1988 - Dies at the age of 69
